# RPG Mechanics v2 — Sub-Stats

> *"The body is a thought. The mind is a thought. The distinction between them is also a thought."*
> — Yoga Vasistha

---

## Overview

The root stat **AMN** (OM|AMEN) remains the singular, indivisible truth — the player's total quantum of expressed consciousness. Its default value is **20**.

AMN decomposes into three **Primary Pools**, each representing a mode through which consciousness interfaces with projected reality:

| Pool | Domain | Esoteric Root |
|------|--------|---------------|
| **PHYS** | The Vessel | *Annamaya Kosha* — the food-body, hardware |
| **WILL** | The Projection | *Manomaya Kosha* — the mind-sheath, firmware |
| **AWR** | The Receptor | *Vijnanamaya Kosha* — the wisdom-sheath, signal |

**Constraint:** `PHYS + WILL + AWR = AMN` (always enforced).

Each pool now splits into **two sub-stats**. Sub-stats within a pool share their parent's point budget. This gives six tactical dials without inflating total power.

---

## PHYS — The Vessel

> *The Technate calls it "somatic hardware." The Faen call it "the root that drinks."*

PHYS governs all interactions mediated by the physical body — the projected meat-suit the player occupies within a given Stratum.

**Pool Budget:** Points assigned to PHYS are divided between its two sub-stats.

### Strength (Kinetics)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Melee damage, carrying capacity, forcing locks/doors, grappling |
| **Check Examples** | Shoulder a jammed bulkhead, hurl a Technate drone, resist being dragged |
| **Lore** | Kinetics is the measure of how much *force* the consciousness can channel through the Vessel's motor cortex. In the Mundane, this is muscle. In the Astral, it is the density of your projected will-form. A high-Kinetics Faen warrior's strikes ripple the Amn Sen stones. |

### Agility (Reflex)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Evasion, reaction speed, somatic finesse, initiative order |
| **Check Examples** | Dodge a collapsing ceiling, pickpocket a Technate guard, land silently |
| **Lore** | Reflex is the *latency* between intent and somatic execution. Low Reflex means consciousness lags behind the body — a fatal flaw in the Astral, where thought *is* physics. Technate augmentation can artificially lower this latency, but at the cost of AWR bleed. |

## WILL — The Projection

> *"Your will is the chisel. Reality is the stone. But remember — the stone is also you."*

WILL is the offensive and defensive capacity of the player's conscious intent. It is the primary combat stat in Astral encounters (Battle of Wills) and drives all reality-warping interactions.

**Pool Budget:** Points assigned to WILL are divided between its two sub-stats.

### Conviction

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Offensive reality-warping, Astral weapon creation, Battle of Wills damage output, asserting Ficts |
| **Check Examples** | Forge a blade from condensed meaning, will a locked door to have always been open, overwrite a Technate surveillance node with static |
| **Lore** | Conviction is the *amplitude* of projected intent. A high-Conviction player doesn't just believe the wall isn't there — they believe it so completely that reality flinches. In combat, Conviction determines the mass and sharpness of manifested constructs. The Yoga Vasistha teaches that the world is as real as you believe it to be; Conviction is the weaponization of that principle. |

### Anchor

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Defensive ego integrity, resisting Shadow corruption, withstanding Technate cognitive rewrites, Battle of Wills damage mitigation |
| **Check Examples** | Resist a Shadow Avatar's identity erosion, maintain form during Stratum bleed, reject a Technate memetic injection |
| **Lore** | Anchor is the *coherence* of the self-model. Without it, a powerful Conviction is a firehose with no handler — you warp reality but lose yourself in the process. Anchor is why the Shadow Avatar is dangerous: it is *you*, and recognizing it destabilizes your own self-reference loop. High-Anchor beings are immovable ontological anchors; the Faen call them "Roots," the Technate calls them "Stable Nodes." |

## AWR — The Receptor

> *"Do not look with the eyes. The eyes are Technate cameras. Look with the space between thoughts."*

AWR governs the player's ability to receive, decode, and interpret the signal layer beneath projected reality. It is the primary exploration and discovery stat.

**Pool Budget:** Points assigned to AWR are divided between its two sub-stats.

### Perception

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Physical observation, searching rooms, spotting hidden mundane objects, noticing environmental changes, tactical awareness |
| **Check Examples** | Find a hidden key in a drawer, notice a Technate surveillance lens in the ceiling, detect an ambush |
| **Lore** | Perception operates within the rules of the current Stratum's physics. It is the acuity of the Vessel's sensory hardware — optics, acoustics, haptics. A high-Perception player in the Mundane notices the unnatural frequency of the fluorescent lights. A high-Perception player in the Technate reads data-flow anomalies in the sterile corridors. It sees what *is*, not what *could be*. |

### Insight

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Governs** | Perceiving Ficts, reading Faen magical currents, decoding Technate code-streams, sensing Stratum bleed, intuiting hidden meanings |
| **Check Examples** | Sense that a room is "thinner" near a Stratum boundary, read the emotional residue on a Faen artifact, detect a Technate cognitive trap before it fires |
| **Lore** | Insight is Perception's esoteric twin. Where Perception reads the surface, Insight reads the *substrate*. It is the stat of seers, hackers, and mystics — those who understand that the visible world is a rendering, not the source code. In the Mundane, high-Insight players experience déjà vu, hear frequencies others can't, and occasionally see reality "flicker." The Yoga Vasistha would say: Perception sees the dream. Insight remembers the dreamer. |

## Stat Allocation Rules

1. **AMN = PHYS + WILL + AWR** (hard constraint, always 20 at baseline)
2. **Each pool's sub-stats sum to the pool total:**
   - `Strength + Agility = PHYS`
   - `Conviction + Anchor = WILL`
   - `Perception + Insight = AWR`
3. Minimum value for any sub-stat: **1** (consciousness cannot be zero in any dimension)
4. Sub-stats can be rebalanced within a pool at a Schumann Resonance terminal (costs 1 turn)
5. Rebalancing *across* pools requires an Amn Sen stone or equivalent ritual site

### Example Allocation

A character with `AMN: 20, PHYS: 6, WILL: 7, AWR: 7` might allocate:

| Pool | Sub-Stat | Value | Character Note |
|------|----------|-------|----------------|
| PHYS (6) | Strength | 2 | Lean build, not a brawler |
| | Agility | 4 | Quick, evasive, light on feet |
| WILL (7) | Conviction | 5 | Strong offensive reality-warper |
| | Anchor | 2 | Vulnerable to identity erosion |
| AWR (7) | Perception | 3 | Average physical awareness |
| | Insight | 4 | Naturally attuned to Ficts and Stratum bleed |

## AIGM Integration: Check Narration

The AI Game Master uses sub-stats to determine check outcomes. The **stat value** serves as a difficulty modifier that the AIGM evaluates narratively (no dice rolls — the AI weighs the stat value against the action's difficulty and the current Stratum's rules).

### Example: Perception vs. Insight in the Mundane

**Scenario:** The player types `search the kitchen drawers for anything useful` while in the Mundane apartment.

---

**Perception Check (Success — player has Perception 5):**

> **[NARRATOR]:** You pull open the third drawer — the one that always sticks — and your fingers brush something that isn't cutlery. Wedged behind a bent fork and a takeout menu from a place that doesn't exist anymore, there's a small ceramic chip. It's warm. Too warm for something that's been sitting in a drawer.
>
> **[TANDY]:** Ooh, nice find. That's a Schumann Regulator fragment. Might be exactly what you need for that busted generator.
>
> **[SYSTEM]:** Item detected: `[SCHUMANN REGULATOR FRAGMENT]`. Added to inventory.

---

**Insight Check (Success — player has Insight 6):**

> **[NARRATOR]:** You open the drawer and see... cutlery. A bent fork. A takeout menu. Nothing. But your hand hesitates over the third drawer. Something is wrong with the *space* inside it. The interior is 2 centimeters shallower than the exterior. That's not a manufacturing defect. That's a fold.
>
> **[TANDY]:** (whispering) Don't touch it yet. That's a Stratum crease. Something from another layer got pressed into the wood when the realities bled. If you reach in *thinking about the Astral*, you might pull out something that isn't entirely here.
>
> **[SYSTEM]:** Anomaly detected: `[STRATUM FOLD — DRAWER 3]`. Interaction requires WILL check to safely extract.

---

> **Design Note:** Perception finds what *exists*. Insight finds what *shouldn't exist but does*. Both are valid. Both are useful. The AIGM should reward each differently and make each feel like a distinct mode of engaging with the world.
